The story of the famous pre-war bakery "Pin Xiang Chuan"

Before the war, when people in Jelebu talked about the three words "Pin Xiang Chuan", they all praised its steamed dumplings, dim sum, wedding pastries and other snacks. It was founded by the founder of the town, Madam Zhan Huan and her husband Mr.Lee, and was next to Wanlicheng General Store.

Due to a fire, the entire row of the old shops was burned down. Later, it moved to Jelebu main street for business. Its descendants, Mr Lee Pei Sen, and his wife Madam Zhu Dong Mei, Mr. Lee Pei Jin and Mr. and Mrs. Yap, opened Xinchengli and Xinjinli coffee shop in Titi.

In the 1970s, it was passed to the third generation, Mr. Lee Chang Jiang, Mr. Lee Wen Rui, Mr. Lee Qing Guang, Mr. Lee Yun Guang and Mr. Lee Sheng Guang, who opened Wenrui Restaurant, Ruifa coffee shop and Sin Swee Fatt Bakery. By the fourth generation, the family's steamed buns, wedding pastries, kaya puffs, siew bao, star cakes and others were famous far and wide. All tourists who came to Titi would come to visit. By the fifth generation, the bakery bussiness moved to Seremban.

The person in the left side of the photo is Madam Lee, also known as Madam Jenn. She is the fourth generation of the bakery and has been running Sin Swee Fatt with her father since she was a child. She started helping her family make steamed buns, wedding pastries, mooncakes and others when she was in primary school, and it has been more than 50 years.

Madam Lee, who has been influenced by her family since she was a child, adheress to very strict standards from the selection of ingredients to the production process and does not accept any mistakes. There's one time, they overcooked the redbean paste and she could not accept it and cook it all over again which the cooking process was very laborious and time consuming. Madam Lee's persistence in the ingredients earn the respect from her customers.

With the persistence of the Lee family, the quality and taste of their delicacy manage to passed down from generation to generation. Many people enjoy this authentic old-fashion taste from then to now.
